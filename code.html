About The Teacup Maltese Puppy

Available Puppies

Overview

The Teacup Maltese Puppy, a small toy breed standing 3 to 6 inches tall and weighing between 2.5 to 5 pounds, has a lifespan of 12 to 15 years and is known for its friendly, gentle, outgoing, and playful temperament, making it a great companion for families, children, seniors, and even other pets like cats and dogs; this breed has a low shedding amount, requires minimal exercise despite its active energy level, and has a frequent vocal tendency, while drooling is rare; characterized by its long white coat, the Maltese is apartment-friendly, easy to train, and ideal for first-time pet owners, although it does require significant grooming and is hypoallergenic, along with exhibiting strong loyalty traits.

Temperament

Gentle, playful, smart, affectionate, and trainable, Maltese dogs are often praised by their admirers as one of the best small breeds available, with Derse noting that they possess a good temperament, being vigorous, cute, and loving; generally agreeable in nature, Maltese dogs get along well with children and adults of all ages, and they can coexist peacefully with cats and other dogs if socialized properly from an early age, making them a great choice for individuals and families alike, although it is essential for parents to teach their children how to appropriately interact with the animals and to always monitor those interactions, especially during the early stages of both the puppy's and child's development.

Appearance

The Teacup Maltese puppy is the ultimate lap dog, characterized by her fluffy white fur, charming black-button nose, dark eyes, and lively personality, which makes her resemble a delightful stuffed animal, as noted by Derse; this breed features a compact, athletic body, small floppy ears, and a tufted tail that arches over her back, and once fully grown, reaching a height of 3 to 6 inches and weight of 2.5 to 5 pounds, her silky smooth white coat requires daily brushing and regular baths to maintain its elegant appearance, while the Maltese is known for low shedding, offering a potentially lower impact on allergy sufferers, though no dog is completely hypoallergenic; owners who intend to show their Maltese typically allow them to keep their long, flowing locks, while many companion owners prefer to opt for frequent trims and shorter hairstyles to simplify grooming and maintenance.
